SPACE TECHNOLOGY

// OVERVIEW

ASTI's Space Technology program is at the forefront of the Philippines' space initiatives. Through the STAMINA4Space Program, we develop indigenous satellite capabilities and leverage space technologies for national development. Our work encompasses the entire lifecycle of small satellite missions from design and fabrication to testing, launch, and operations.

The program supports critical national needs in disaster management, agriculture monitoring, environmental assessment, and maritime domain awareness through satellite-based Earth observation and data analytics.

// KEY PROJECTS

  • DIWATA Microsatellite Series - Earth observation microsatellites developed in partnership with Hokkaido University and Tohoku University. DIWATA-1 was launched in 2016, making the Philippines one of few Southeast Asian countries with Earth observation capability.
  • Maya CubeSat Series - 1U CubeSats developed with Philippine universities through the Space Technology Capacity Building Program. Maya-1 (2018), Maya-2 (2021), Maya-3 (2022), and Maya-4 (2022) are operational.
  • Philippine Earth Data Observation and Research (PEDRO) Center - Ground station facility for receiving and processing satellite data. Located at ASTI, it provides downlink services for various satellite missions.
  • GeoSAIL: Geospatial Science and AI Lab - AI-powered satellite image analysis for land cover classification, change detection, and disaster assessment.

// CAPABILITIES

  • Satellite Assembly and Integration Facility
  • Thermal Vacuum Testing Chamber
  • Vibration Testing Equipment
  • Ground Control Station with S-band and X-band capability
  • Satellite Data Processing and Analysis Platform

// COLLABORATIONS

ASTI collaborates with international space agencies and research institutions including:

  • Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA)
  • Hokkaido University and Tohoku University (Japan)
  • National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)
  • European Space Agency (ESA)
  • ASEAN space agencies and regional partners

// DATA ACCESS

Satellite data and imagery products are available through:

  • PEDRO Data Portal
  • PhilSpace Geoportal
  • ASEAN Emergency Response Data
